# Annex G — After Action Review (AAR) Template

Used at [Lessons Learned](../06-lessons-learned.md), battle procedure step 16. Structured
military AAR format — not a vague "what went wrong" conversation.

```
AFTER ACTION REVIEW — [Incident ID]
Date of AAR: [date]        Facilitator: [name]
Participants: [list]

1. WHAT WAS SUPPOSED TO HAPPEN
   [Plan as written / expected sequence]

2. WHAT ACTUALLY HAPPENED
   [Factual timeline. Avoid blame language]

3. WHY THE DIFFERENCE
   [Root cause: process / tooling / people / plan itself]

4. SUSTAIN
   [What worked  (do not change)]

5. IMPROVE
   [Action item] — Owner: [name] — Due: [date]
   [Action item] — Owner: [name] — Due: [date]
```

## Facilitation Notes

- Keep step 2 factual and blame-free. The goal is an accurate timeline, not assigning fault
- Every item in step 5 needs a named owner and a date, or it will not get done
- Feed metrics (MTTD/MTTC/MTTE/MTTR) into this review to track trend over time, not just
  this incident in isolation
